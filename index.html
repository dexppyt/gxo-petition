GXO Lelystad – Collective Employee Petition

To: HR Team, GXO Logistics

Subject: Formal objection to proposed contract changes and calendar restructuring

We are writing to you as a collective group of employees from various departments and job categories within GXO Logistics at the Lelystad Platform. We have all received the proposal regarding the new calendar layout implementation effective as of July 1st, 2026.

First and foremost, we want to voice our collective outrage and deep disagreement regarding the manner in which this situation is being handled. Receiving this notification on May 18th and forcing us to make a binding decision in the portal by June 1st is an absolute lack of respect toward us as employees. A modification of core contractual terms (primaire arbeidsvoorwaarden), such as lowering our permanent contracted hours, is a major decision that impacts our life and financial stability. To demand a choice within a two-week window, without giving us proper time to seek thorough external legal counsel, is highly precipitate and unacceptable.

Therefore, through this document, we formally communicate that we completely reject the proposal to reduce our current 39.1, 37.4, or 35.7 contracted hours. None of the undersigned employees will select the 35.2 or the 36.7-hour options in the portal, nor will we sign any individual amendments. Our collective refusal is based on the fact that the current offer of adding three scale steps (stappen/tredes) is completely inefficient and unacceptable due to the following structural damages it causes:

  • Lack of financial transparency: No clear calculations showing compensation for salary loss.
  • Career progression trap: Promotion to higher job category likely removes benefit of added scale steps.
  • Reduced mortgage capacity (Hypotheek): Lower contracted hours reduce borrowing power in the Netherlands.
  • Loss of overtime premiums (Overuren): Extra hours will be paid at 100% instead of 130%.
  • Reduction of vacation and IKB hours: Lower contract reduces holiday accrual and flexibility budget.
  • Impact on holiday allowance: 8% allowance decreases due to lower gross annual salary.
  • Social security and sick pay risks: WW and Ziektewet calculations negatively affected.
  • Reduction in pension capital: Lower contributions to Pensioenfonds Vervoer long-term.
  • Work-life balance concerns: Shift rigidity increases while financial security decreases.

While we note that the Works Council (OR) aligned on the operational layout of the calendar shifts, the OR has absolutely no legal authority over our individual primary terms of employment. Under Dutch labor law (Section 7:611 and 7:613 of the Dutch Civil Code), an employer cannot force a structural salary and hour reduction without the explicit, voluntary consent of the employee, or without ensuring a proper phased-out scheme (afbouwregeling).

We do not wish to block GXO's operational efficiency, but we demand to be treated with the respect we deserve. We request an immediate collective negotiation meeting with HR to discuss a truly fair compensation model and proper guarantees before any steps are taken in the portal.

We expect a prompt response acknowledging this collective position and establishing a meeting date.
