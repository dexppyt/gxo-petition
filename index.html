GXO Lelystad – Collective Petition

Dear HR Team, We are writing to you as a collective group of employees from various departments and job categories within GXO Logistics at the Lelystad Platform. We have all received the proposal regarding the new calendar layout implementation effective as of July 1st, 2026. First and foremost, we want to voice our collective outrage and deep disagreement regarding the manner in which this situation is being handled. Receiving this notification on May 18th and forcing us to make a binding decision in the portal by June 1st is an absolute lack of respect toward us as employees. A modification of core contractual terms (primaire arbeidsvoorwaarden), such as lowering our permanent contracted hours, is a major decision that impacts our life and financial stability. To demand a choice within a two-week window, without giving us proper time to seek thorough external legal counsel, is highly precipitate and unacceptable. Therefore, through this document, we formally communicate that we completely reject the proposal to reduce our current 39.1, 37.4, or 35.7 contracted hours. None of the undersigned employees will select the 35.2 or the 36.7-hour options in the portal, nor will we sign any individual amendments. Our collective refusal is based on the fact that the current offer of adding three scale steps (stappen/tredes) is completely inefficient and unacceptable due to the following extensive list of structural damages it causes us: Lack of financial proof and transparency: The company has failed to demonstrate through clear, individual calculations how these three steps will actually compensate for the net structural salary loss. The career progression trap (Promotions): According to GXO’s grading policies, when an employee promotes to a higher job category, they move into a different salary scale. Consequently, it is highly probable that we will completely lose the benefit of these three artificial steps upon promotion, as our salary will simply be adjusted to the standard entry levels of the new scale. The compensation disappears the moment we grow within the company. Severe Reduction in Borrowing and Mortgage Capacity (Hypotheek): Financial institutions and banks in the Netherlands strictly evaluate gross annual contract income and fixed contract hours. A permanent, structural drop in contract hours drastically lowers our maximum mortgage capacity, directly sabotaging our ability to buy a home or secure credit in this country. Loss of Overtime Premiums (Overuren): Lowering our contracts means that extra hours worked up to 40 hours will be compensated as standard "additional hours" (meer-uren) at 100%. Under our current contracts, we reach the 130% overtime premium almost immediately. With the new proposal, we will be forced to work operational peaks for less premium pay. Proportionate Reduction of Vacation and IKB Hours: According to our agreements, holiday hours are built up strictly in proportion to contract hours. A drop in our contractual hours directly reduces our annual vacation buildup and shrinks our Individueel Keuze Budget (IKB), stripping away our flexibility to either take time off or sell those hours for extra cash. Impact on Holiday Allowance (Vakantietoeslag): Since the mandatory 8% holiday allowance is directly tied to our structural gross annual contract salary, our overall annual payout will be permanently diminished. Social Security and Sick Pay Risks: Any future unemployment benefits (WW-uitkering) or long-term sick pay (Ziektewet) are calculated based on contracted hours and daily wage (dagloon). This change permanently compromises our financial safety net under Dutch law. Reduction in Pension Capital: Our long-term retirement build-up is directly impacted by our part-time factor. A lower contract means less capital accumulated in our pension fund (Pensioenfonds Vervoer) every month, creating a lifetime deficit that temporary scale increases cannot fix. Detriment to Work-Life Balance: The presentation states this change aims to improve work-life balance. However, forcing us into rigid shift structures while permanently slashing our contractual economic security achieves the exact opposite, destabilizing our personal and family planning. While we note that the Works Council (OR) aligned on the operational layout of the calendar shifts, the OR has absolutely no legal authority over our individual primary terms of employment. Under Dutch labor law (Section 7:611 and 7:613 of the Dutch Civil Code), an employer cannot force a structural salary and hour reduction without the explicit, voluntary consent of the employee, or without ensuring a sound phased-out scheme (afbouwregeling). We do not wish to block GXO's operational efficiency, but we demand to be treated with the respect we deserve. We request an immediate collective negotiation meeting with HR to discuss a truly fair compensation model and proper guarantees before any steps are taken in the portal. We expect a prompt response acknowledging this collective position and establishing a meeting date. Sincerely, The Undersigned GXO Employees / Lelystad Platform
